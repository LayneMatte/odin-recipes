Crawfish Etouffee!

Description

Crawfish Etouffee is a classic Louisiana dish with a buttery, rich, and intensely flavorful sauce and heaps of fresh crawfish tails, herbs, and spices. This fabulous dish’s secret is a good roux and the holy trinity with extra garlic thrown in. It’s guaranteed to have everyone asking for seconds or even thirds!

Yields: Eight Servings

Preparation time: 45 minutes

Cooking time: 1 hour and 10 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 pound butter or margarine
  • 5 1/2 pounds chopped onions
  • 4 cups chopped green bell peppers
  • 4 cups parsley, stems removed, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Louisiana hot sauce (or 1/2 teaspoon Tabasco)
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 4 teaspoons salt (adding 1 teaspoon at a time to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 5 to 6 pounds live or partly cooked whole crawfishv

Directions

  1. Put butter into very large heavy-bottomed stockpot; melt over medium heat. (Use crawfish fat if you have it.) Add onions, peppers, parsley, hot sauce and Worcestershire sauce. Stir in 1 teaspoon salt and lemon juice. Simmer, covered, stirring often, for 1 hour. (This will make its own juice.)

  2. Taste and adjust seasonings, adding more salt if necessary. Add the crawfish. Cook, covered, until crawfish turn red and tail meat is cooked through, about 10 to 20 minutes. Let cool slightly.

  3. To eat, use fingers to separate head from tail. Peel back tail shell and eat tail meat. Serve with plenty of napkins.
